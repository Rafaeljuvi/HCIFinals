The lateral raise is a shoulder exercise that primarily targets the middle deltoid, while also engaging the anterior and posterior deltoids, as well as the trapezius and rotator cuff muscles. By lifting the arms out to the sides, the lateral raise effectively strengthens and tones the shoulder muscles, promoting improved shoulder stability, range of motion, and overall upper body strength.

  1. Stand tall with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  2. Keep your core engaged and maintain a slight bend in your elbows.
  3. With control, lift your arms out to the sides while keeping them parallel to the ground.
  4. Ensure that your palms are facing the ground and that your elbows are slightly higher than your wrists.
  5. Hold the raised position briefly, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form and avoiding swinging or using momentum.

The Machine shoulder press is a compound exercise that targets multiple muscles in the upper body. It primarily focuses on the deltoid muscles, specifically the anterior deltoids, which are located on the front of the shoulders.

  1. Sit on the machine, adjusting the seat height so that the handles are at shoulder level.
  2. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Push the handles upward by extending your arms fully.
  4. Pause briefly at the top, with your arms extended but not locked.
  5. Lower the handles back down to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining controlled movements throughout.

The seated military press, also known as the seated overhead press, is an effective compound exercise that primarily targets the deltoid muscles, particularly the anterior deltoids.

  1. Sit on a bench or chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Hold a barbell or dumbbells at shoulder level with your palms facing forward.
  3. Press the weight overhead, fully extending your arms without locking your elbows.
  4. Lower the weight back down to shoulder level.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

The front raise is an exercise that primarily targets the anterior deltoid muscles, which are located on the front of the shoulders.In addition to the anterior deltoids, the front raise also engages the lateral deltoids and the upper portion of the pectoral muscles to a lesser extent.

  1. Stand tall with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with an overhand grip.
  2. Keep your arms straight or slightly bent at the elbows.
  3. Raise the weights directly in front of you, maintaining control and avoiding swinging.
  4. Continue lifting until your arms are parallel to the ground or slightly higher.
  5. Lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, focusing on proper form and controlled movements throughout.
