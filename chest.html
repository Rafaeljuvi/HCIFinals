Bench press is a compound movement that targeted more than one muscle. You must include bench press into your push or chest routine. Targeted muscle is the chest muscles (pectoralis major and minor), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps.

  1. Lie on the bench, pull your shoulder blades together and down, and slightly arch your back.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Inhale, hold your breath, and unrack the bar.
  4. Lower the bar with control, until it touches your chest somewhere close to your sternum.
  5. Push the bar up to the starting position while exhaling.
  6. Take another breath while in the top position, and repeat for reps.

This variation of the traditional bench press primarily targets the upper chest muscles, shoulders, and triceps. By adjusting the incline angle, individuals can customize the emphasis on different muscle groups.

  1. Set up an incline bench at your desired angle (usually between 30-45 degrees).
  2. Position yourself on the bench with your feet firmly planted on the floor.
  3. Lie back on the bench and grasp the barbell with a grip slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  4. Lift the barbell off the rack, ensuring your arms are fully extended.
  5. Lower the barbell slowly and under control towards your upper chest, keeping your elbows slightly tucked in.
  6. Pause for a moment when the barbell touches your chest, and then press it back up to the starting position by extending your arms.

The pec deck, also known as the chest fly machine, is a popular exercise equipment found in gyms that specifically targets the muscles of the chest, primarily the pectoralis major. It consists of a seat and two vertical handles that are attached to a weight stack.

  1. Select the weight for the machine.
  2. Sit on the platform. Press your back firmly against the back of the platform with your feet flat on the floor.

The cable fly is an effective exercise that targets the chest muscles, primarily the pectoralis major. To perform the cable fly, begin by adjusting the pulleys on a cable machine to a height slightly higher than shoulder level.

  1. Set both pulleys directly at (or slightly above) shoulder height and select the desired weight.
  2. Grasp both handles with a neutral grip and take a step forward to split the stance.
  3. Press the handles to lockout while flexing the pecs and extending the elbows.
  4. Keep a slight bend in the elbows, move entirely at the shoulder joint, and slowly allow the arms to open while the pecs stretch.
  5. Return to the starting position by flexing your pecs and bringing the handles together at chest height.
  6. Slowly lower back to the starting position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
