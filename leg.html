The squat is a fundamental compound exercise that primarily targets the muscles of the lower body, particularly the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. As you lower your body into a squat position, the quadriceps muscles at the front of your thighs contract to extend the knee joint, while the hamstrings at the back of your thighs engage to control the descent and provide stability.

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  2. Place the barbell on your upper back, resting it comfortably on your traps.
  3. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  4. Keep your chest up, engage your core, and maintain a neutral spine.
  5. Begin the squat by pushing your hips back and bending your knees.
  6. Descend until your thighs are parallel to the ground, or lower if comfortable.
  7. Keep your knees in line with your toes and your weight on your heels.
  8. Drive through your heels to stand back up, fully extending your hips and knees.
  9. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

The leg press is a versatile and effective strength training exercise that targets multiple muscles in the lower body. The leg press primarily targets the muscles of the lower body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes.

  1. Sit on the leg press machine with your back against the backrest.
  2. Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform.
  3. Bend your knees and lower the platform towards your body.
  4. Push the platform away by extending your legs.
  5. Fully extend your legs without locking your knees.
  6. Lower the platform back to the starting position.
  7. Repeat for desired repetitions.

The hip thrust is a powerful exercise specifically designed to target and activate the gluteal muscles, with a primary focus on the gluteus maximus. By positioning the body in a way that optimally engages these muscles, the hip thrust promotes maximum gluteal activation and development.

  1. Sit on the ground with your upper back against a bench.
  2. Bend your knees and place your feet flat on the floor.
  3. Position a barbell or weight across your hips.
  4. Drive through your heels and lift your hips off the ground.
  5. Extend your hips until your body forms a straight line.
  6. Lower your hips back to the starting position.
  7. Repeat for desired repetitions.

Lunges primarily target the quadriceps, hamstrings, and gluteal muscles, while also engaging the calves, core muscles, and ankle stabilizers. They are an effective exercise for developing lower body strength, stability, and muscle tone.

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart.
  2. Take a step forward with one leg, lowering your body into a lunge position.
  3. Keep your front knee aligned with your ankle, and your back knee hovering just above the ground.
  4. Push through your front heel to return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat on the other leg, alternating sides.
  6. Continue alternating lunges for the desired number of repetitions.
