The Lat Pulldown exercise is a highly effective compound movement that targets the latissimus dorsi, or "lats," which are the broad muscles of the back. By gripping the bar overhead and pulling it down towards the chest, the lat pulldown engages and strengthens the lats, along with other supporting muscles such as the rhomboids, biceps, and rear deltoids.

  1. Set your grip around shoulder width distance. Your hands should act like hooks, so no need to grip the bar too tightly.her and down, and slightly arch your back.
  2. Hold your core tight, internally rotate your hands against the bar and pull your shoulder blades down and back. Breathe in.
  3. Breathing out and keeping your shoulder blades retracted, pull the bar down to the top of your sternum (chest bone).
  4. At the low point of the move, you should pause for 1 second and squeeze, before lowering the weight under full, slow control.
  5. Repeat for the required number of reps, staying true to the form throughout.

Pull-ups are a challenging and highly effective compound exercise that primarily targets the muscles of the upper body, including the latissimus dorsi (lats), rhomboids, trapezius, biceps brachii, and brachialis. Additionally, the exercise engages the muscles of the shoulders, specifically the deltoids, and the muscles of the arms, such as the forearms and grip muscles.

  1. Find a sturdy pull-up bar and grip it with slightly wider than shoulder-width overhand grip.
  2. Hang from the bar with arms fully extended and engage your core muscles.
  3. Pull your body up by driving your elbows down and back, using your back muscles. Aim to bring your chin above the bar.
  4. Pause briefly at the top, then slowly lower yourself back down with control.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps, focusing on proper form and engaging the back muscles.

The dumbbell row is a compound exercise that targets the muscles of the upper back, including the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, and trapezius, while also engaging the biceps and forearms.

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding dumbbells in each hand with a neutral grip.
  2. Hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and parallel to the ground.
  3. Pull one dumbbell up towards your hip, keeping your elbow close to your body and squeezing your back muscles.
  4. Pause briefly at the top, then lower the dumbbell back down in a controlled manner.
  5. Alternate sides and repeat for the desired number of reps.

The seated cable row is a compound exercise that targets the muscles of the upper back, including the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, and rear deltoids, while also engaging the biceps and forearms.

  1. Sit on the rowing machine or bench, placing your feet on the footplates or platform.
  2. Grab the V-bar attachment with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart.
  3. Sit up straight, engage your core, and pull the V-bar towards your abdomen, squeezing your back muscles.
  4. Pause briefly at the end of the movement, then slowly release the V-bar back to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps, maintaining proper form and control.
